# Federated delivery: self-serve baseline + champions, not central cohorts

The audience is ~1000 DSQ across 15–20 departments. Central staff cannot run cohorts at that
scale, and most small departments will never get a formal instructor-led cohort.

**Decision.** Delivery is **federated**. The central program owns the **Spine**, the **Self-serve**
entry site, the official-course reading lists, the **Demo library**, and **Champion enablement**
(train-the-trainer). Delivery is decentralized:

- **Self-serve** is the universal baseline for all ~1000 DSQ (reaches the long tail).
- Each department's **Champion** sustains adoption, runs office hours, and curates **Contributed
  demos**.
- **Cohorts** are department-run — instructor-led in the big departments, Champion-/volunteer-led
  elsewhere. Central staff do **not** run cohorts for every department.

## Considered options

- **Central-facilitator-led cohorts for everyone** — rejected: facilitator bottleneck by ~the
  4th department; cannot reach 1000 Learners or the small-department long tail.

## Consequences

- The program's deliverables tilt toward **enabling Champions** (a champion / train-the-trainer
  kit) over running classes. The existing `docs/facilitator-guide.md` is really the
  Champion/Facilitator runbook and should read that way.
- **Self-serve quality is load-bearing**, not a nice-to-have — the entry site is the baseline
  experience for most Learners.
- Needs a Champion **selection + enablement path** (largely an org concern, flagged here as a
  dependency, not solved in this repo).
